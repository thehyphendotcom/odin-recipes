Tacos al Pastor

Description

Tacos al Pastor are a popular Mexican dish made with marinated pork that is cooked on a vertical spit, similar to shawarma. The pork is typically marinated in a blend of dried chilies, spices, and pineapple juice, giving it a unique and flavorful taste. The meat is thinly sliced and served on corn tortillas, often garnished with chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.

Ingredients

  • Pork shoulder
  • Dried chilies (such as guajillo and ancho)
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Pineapple juice
  • Vinegar
  • Spices (such as cumin, oregano, and cloves)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Corn tortillas
  • Optional toppings: chopped onions, cilantro, lime wedges, and pineapple chunks

Steps

  1. Marinate the pork shoulder with a blend of dried chilies, garlic, onions, pineapple juice, vinegar, spices, salt, and pepper. Let it sit for at least a few hours or overnight for best results.
  2. Cook the marinated pork on a vertical spit or grill until it is cooked through and slightly charred on the edges. If you don't have a spit, you can also cook it in a skillet or oven.
  3. Once the pork is cooked, thinly slice it and set it aside.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas and fill them with the sliced pork.
  5. Serve the tacos with optional toppings like chopped onions, cilantro, lime wedges, and pineapple chunks for added flavor.
